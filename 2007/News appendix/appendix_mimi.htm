Local High School Student Wins Third Place in "Drawing the Line on Gun Violence" National Poster Contest

Contest Commemorates Third Anniversary of Columbine Tragedy

Portland, OR ¡ª Today national youth anti-gun violence group Co/Motion and Former U.S. Representative Elizabeth Furse, presented an award to a winner of the "Drawing the Line on Gun Violence" National Poster Contest at Portland State University. Mimi Yan of Tigard was awarded third place from more than 1,000 entries from around the country. Mimi, who moved to Tigard from China when she was five years old, is a 16-year old junior at Jiao Ying Chinese Culture and Art School. She has received the President's award for service and is a member of the National Honor Society and Key Club. Mimi, who is a member of a school dance group, also teaches children Chinese cultural dance.

Mimi, who has won a number of awards for her artwork, said she "hopes to create more works of art to impact a safer environment to make the world a better place for everyone." In her poster, she wanted to illustrate that "guns can hurt anyone no matter how young or old." Mimi will travel to Washington, DC, for a reception honoring the 13 winners and their teachers on Capitol Hill on Tuesday, April 23rd.

Alliance for Justice President Nan Aron said, "Nine young people a day die due to gun violence. The 'Drawing the Line on Gun Violence' Student Poster Contest takes a step toward empowering young people to stand against gun violence in their communities. The tremendous turnout is a statement of the students' concern about the threat of gun violence. Mimi's artwork stood out among the rest for its artistry and powerful message."

Mimi said that she was strongly motivated to enter the contest because she "has felt the terror and tragedy of gun violence by always hearing news of deaths and other violent acts with guns." For this reason, Mimi chose to draw numerous faces to represent the many victims gun violence.

Congressman David Wu presented the award to Mimi Yan (middle) and congratulated to her teacher Jiao Ying on April 23, 2002.
